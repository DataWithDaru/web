Tableau

According to Gartner Tableau and PowerBI are the two main leaders in the business intelligence plaform market. Tableau was introduced in 2003 and grew into one of the most popular BI tools. The question would be, why Tableau over PowerBI? The reality of the situation is they do majority of things the same, they have beautiful visualizations, they integrate with various data sources, they are user friendly, easy to integrate with other applications. My approach is simple: Not all clients need the same thing, therefore, I need to be flexible in what I am capable of doing, however, I would still call myself a PowerBI enthusiast. Nevertheless, take a look!

Power BI

PowerBI has been the cool kid on the block since Microsoft launched it in 2011 and added it to Office 365 in 2013. By the time I was introduced to the industry of business intelligence in 2019, I noticed how it just stood out from the rest of it's competitors but the majority of our clients at that time did not adopt it, yet. PowerBI is in my opinion the leading BI solution to the majority of the companies out there, we recently started adopting it in our workspace as well, once you see the potential, it's hard to go back, especially if most people are already onboard themselves. It integrates seamlessly with so many applications, it has great AI capabilities, it's secure, it's beatiful and is definitely worth looking into if you have not already done so.

Paginated Reports

Paginated reports are something every company is or should be using. PowerBI has some stunning report features but sometimes that is just not what's required. In the industry of insurance, you need to understand where the money is going, how the brokers are doing, and that's great for using Power BI Desktop, but you also need to print policy schedules; that's where Report Builder/SSRS comes into play. Usually, these kinds of reports have sensitive data that one cannot make known to the general public, therefore I have decided to show you Microsoft's secure embed option.
